Basic Elements & Structural Tags

HTML tags act like container brackets around texts. They tell the browser whether a piece of text is a big title, a regular paragraph, or a button.

1. Headings (<h1> to <h6>)

Headings are used to establish clean hierarchies on your page. <h1> is the biggest, most important title, while <h2> is a smaller sub-section.

2. Paragraphs (<p>)

The <p> tag is used for regular blocks of text. Every time you open a new paragraph tag, the browser automatically jumps to a new line.
